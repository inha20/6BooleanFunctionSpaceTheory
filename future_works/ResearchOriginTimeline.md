# Research Origin Timeline

## Purpose

This document records major moments in which structural patterns, invariance, symmetry, or meaningful relationships were perceived during the development of the research program.

---

## Elementary School

### Multiplication Table

Observation:

Repeated addition creates predictable numerical structures.

Research significance:

Early recognition that patterns can emerge from simple rules.

---

## Middle School

### Algebraic Identities

Observation:

Expansion formulas can be understood through geometric area models and tabular structures.

### Euler Characteristic

Observation:

For polyhedra:

V - E + F = 2

A hidden invariant appears across different shapes.

---

## High School

### Function Construction Problems

Observation:

Multiple conditions can be integrated into a single coherent structure.

### Genetics and Pascal's Triangle

Observation:

Probability structures appear naturally from independent inheritance.

### Symmetric Boolean Functions

Observation:

Some functions remain unchanged under variable exchange.

---

## University

### Karnaugh Maps

Observation:

Certain Boolean functions generate recognizable visual patterns.

Examples:

- Checkerboard
- Corner
- Ring
- Block
- Diagonal

This became the starting point of the visual pattern research program.

---

## Research Stage

### Variable Rearrangement Invariance

Observation:

Visual appearance changes, but some structural properties remain.

### Equivalence Class Question

Observation:

24 variable arrangements may not represent 24 fundamentally different structures.

### Structure Recognition Theory

Observation:

Many research projects appear connected by a common theme:

How are meaningful structures recognized?
