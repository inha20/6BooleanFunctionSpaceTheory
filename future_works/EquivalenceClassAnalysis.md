# Variable Arrangement Equivalence Classes

## Research Motivation

The current thesis analyzes pattern transformations under variable rearrangement.

However, an additional observation emerged during the research process:

Although there are 24 possible variable permutations for a 4-variable Karnaugh map, many of them appear to generate equivalent visual structures.

This raises the following question:

Can the 24 arrangements be classified into a smaller number of representative arrangement classes?

---

## Preliminary Observation

Observation:

24 variable arrangements do not appear to generate 24 fundamentally different visual structures.

Instead, many arrangements produce visually equivalent patterns under rotation, reflection, or coordinate relabeling.

Hypothesis:

The 24 arrangements may be grouped into a small number of equivalence classes.

Current working observation:

24 arrangements → approximately 3 representative arrangement groups

This observation requires systematic verification.

---

## Related Research Questions

- Why do some variable exchanges preserve pattern structure?
- Which permutations belong to the same equivalence class?

- Can equivalence classes be described using the Symmetric Group S₄?
