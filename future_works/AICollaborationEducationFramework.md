# AI Collaboration Education Framework

## Motivation

AI education is often focused on tool usage.

However, effective human-AI collaboration may require a different set of skills beyond operating AI systems.

This document records a framework distinguishing AI usage education from AI collaboration education.

---

## Two Educational Tracks

### 1. AI Usage Education

Focus:

- Using AI tools
- Prompt writing
- Feature utilization
- Model capabilities and limitations
- Practical productivity workflows

Typical question:

"How do I use AI effectively?"

---

### 2. AI Collaboration Education

Focus:

- Question generation
- Problem framing
- Role division between human and AI
- Critical evaluation of AI outputs
- Context management
- Research collaboration workflows

Typical question:

"How do humans and AI work together effectively?"

---

## Working Hypothesis

Current educational programs often emphasize AI usage skills.

However, long-term value may increasingly depend on collaboration skills.

As AI systems become more capable, the ability to design questions, evaluate outputs, preserve context, and manage collaboration may become increasingly important.

---

## Relationship to Human-AI Collaboration

Possible division of labor:

Human:
- Goal selection
- Context preservation
- Question generation
- Judgment and evaluation

AI:
- Analysis
- Retrieval
- Draft generation
- Pattern exploration

Education may need to explicitly teach this division of labor.
