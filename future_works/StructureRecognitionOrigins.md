# Structure Recognition Origins

## Central Question

Why do some phenomena appear worthy of investigation?

This document records a possible research direction that emerged from studies on Karnaugh map patterns, symmetry, invariance, and human-AI collaboration.

---

## Proposed Progression

Pattern
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning
→ Question
→ Research
→ New Structure

Research may begin when a person notices a pattern that appears non-random and worthy of explanation.

---

## Working Hypothesis

Humans do not investigate every pattern they encounter.

Instead, certain patterns attract attention, appear meaningful, and generate questions.

Those questions may become research projects.

The mechanism that transforms observed patterns into research questions is currently unknown.

---

## Connections to Existing Research

### Karnaugh Map Visual Patterns

Observation of checkerboards, rings, corners, and diagonal structures.

### Symmetric Boolean Functions

Observation that visual structures emerge from Hamming-weight layers.

### Variable Rearrangement Invariance

Observation that some structures persist despite transformation.

### Human-AI Collaboration

Observation that humans often identify phenomena while AI helps generate explanatory frameworks.

## Research Questions

- Why do some patterns attract attention?
- Why do some structures appear non-random?
- How are research questions generated?
- Is curiosity related to structure recognition?
- Can AI participate in structure discovery?
